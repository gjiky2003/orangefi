## 1.1 Executive Summary

After analyzing 10 lending niches across TAM, competitive intensity, regulatory complexity, unit economics, and solo-founder feasibility — **the recommended niche is debt consolidation loans ($5k–$35k) originated through a bank partnership model.**

**Why debt consolidation:**
- Large, proven TAM ($650B U.S. consumer debt)
- Clear value proposition (save money on interest)
- Banks actively seek consolidation partners (improves their portfolio quality)
- Low fraud relative to small-dollar or no-doc loans
- AI can meaningfully differentiate underwriting
- Strong fee-based revenue (2-5% origination + servicing)

**Runner-up:** Home improvement loans (growing TAM, green energy tailwinds)

## 1.5 Regulatory Complexity Assessment

### Federal Regulations

| Regulation | Applicability | Complexity |
|------------|--------------|------------|
| TILA (Reg Z) | Full — all consumer lending | Moderate |
| ECOA (Reg B) | Full — adverse action, fair lending | Moderate |
| FCRA | Full — credit reporting, disputes | Moderate |
| UDAAP | Full — CFPB enforcement risk | High |
| Truth in Lending Act | Full — APR disclosures | Moderate |
| Equal Credit Opportunity Act | Full — application rules | Moderate |
| Gramm-Leach-Bliley (GLBA) | Full — privacy, data security | Moderate |
| Bank Secrecy Act/AML | Indirect — via bank partner | Low |
| Military Lending Act | Applicable — 36% rate cap | Low |

### State Regulations

| Requirement | Complexity | Notes |
|-------------|------------|-------|
| State lending licenses | High | 50-state analysis needed |
| Rate caps (usury) | Moderate | Range from 6% (MN) to no cap |
| UCCC compliance | Moderate | Uniform Consumer Credit Code states |
| Collection licensing | Moderate | State-by-state |

### Bank Partnership Advantage
Because the bank originates and holds loans, many state licensing requirements may be preempted under federal banking law (National Bank Act, Depository Institutions Deregulation Act). This is the SINGLE biggest regulatory advantage of the bank partnership model — you operate under the bank's regulatory umbrella, not 50 separate state regimes.
